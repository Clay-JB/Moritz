Rhythm of Triverz is a soulslike rhythm-action game set in medieval Austria. It was created in a team of 6 in the span of half a year. Leo Sametinger and I formed the sound team, composing and recording the soundtrack, the sound effects and ambience. We wrote, directed, recorded and starred in the voice acting.

I also worked closely with the 3D modeller Mia Cain, creating texture paints and sketches for character models to work off of. I painted the inventory icons and some simple 2.5D assets, created splash art and credit art.

For the music, we came up with system to support the rhythmic combat aspect while still being adaptive. I composed three variants for each section in the soundtrack, one tension building variant that plays when enemies approach (“unsheathe”), one more intense variant for general combat (“Ernst auf Ernst”) which evolves further when the player hits on beat. I used FMOD for this as well as the ambience, which transitions between different stages for different parameters, allowing for highly specific environment sounds to be used very easily.

For the illustrations of the inventory items, I mostly used Krita and Procreate. Some of my 2D illustrations can also be found on 3D objects in the level, like wall murals and shop signs.
